The Problem
Military readiness tracking was broken.
📋
Problem 01
Manual & Error-Prone
Squadron leaders tracked personnel in spreadsheets — no live updates, high risk of outdated or overwritten data with every change.
👁️
Problem 02
No Readiness Visibility
Commanders had no instant view of available vs. TDY, medical, or deployed personnel. They had to count it manually, every time.
🔓
Problem 03
No Access Control
Shared files gave everyone equal edit rights. No role separation — one wrong click could overwrite critical manning data.
45 min
Average time for a single manual update cycle
Twice a week — over 70 hours of leadership time lost per year, per squadron.
Background & Literature
Grounded in doctrine and research.
Operational Context
Military personnel readiness tracking is a mission-critical function. Incorrect or stale manning data directly affects sortie generation, weapons load planning, and force protection decisions.

Existing DoD solutions (MICT, LIMS-EV) are network-dependent, slow to update, and provide no visual drag-and-drop interface for rapid reassignment.

SMRVT was designed to fill the gap at the squadron level — where decisions happen quickly and network access is not always guaranteed.
Supporting Literature
Offline-First Design
PouchDB / CouchDB replication (Apache, 2023) — persistent local storage with optional remote sync, meeting DoD self-contained environment requirements.
Access Control
NIST SP 800-53 AC-2 / AC-3 — least-privilege principles enforced by restricting structural edits to authorized admin accounts only.
Readiness Metrics
AFI 10-201 (Force Readiness Reporting) — defines how TDY, Medical, Leave, and Deployed statuses are excluded from unit readiness calculations.
Design & Development Approach
Five phases. One working system.
01
Requirements
Gathered from USAF munitions unit workflows. Defined role separation, offline-first capability, and readiness calculation logic.
02
Architecture
Modular ES6 split: main.js · state.js · config.js · dragDrop.js · metrics.js · importExport.js
03
Development
Iterative 5-person build. PouchDB persistence, drag-and-drop swap logic, 20-step undo stack, What-If mode.
04
Integration
PouchDB ↔ IndexedDB local. Optional CouchDB / Cloudant bidirectional replication for multi-user sync.
05
Testing
10 structured test cases: functional, usability, data validation, integration, and regression. All 10 passed.
Key Features
Every feature maps to a stated requirement.
🖱️
Drag & Drop Assignment
Move personnel between section slots with automatic swap logic and instant readiness recalculation.
🔒
Role-Based Access Control
Admin / User accounts with session persistence. Structural edits restricted to authorized admin only.
💾
Offline-First PouchDB
All data persists locally in IndexedDB. No server or internet connection required during operation.
📊
Live Readiness Metrics
Centralized metrics engine calculates manning % and readiness % across all sections in real time.
☁️
Remote CouchDB Sync
Optional bidirectional sync with CouchDB or IBM Cloudant — live updates across multiple clients.
📄
CSV & PDF Export
Full roster CSV export for backup and re-import. Landscape PDF report with metrics and full personnel summary.

Welcome to the live demonstration of SMRVT. What you are seeing on screen is the actual application running in a browser — no mockup, no slides. We are starting at the login screen. I am logging in as admin, which gives full access to every feature of the board. Once authenticated, the dashboard loads instantly with our full personnel dataset already populated. If a slot is already filled, the two cards swap positions with no data loss. Now I am going to add a brand new personnel record. I fill in the name, rank, AFSC, and set the status to TDY. When I save, the card appears on the board and the readiness count adjusts immediately to reflect that this person is unavailable. Over here is the deployed panel. Personnel flagged as deployed are tracked separately with their expected DEROS return date, so commanders always know who is downrange and when they are coming back. Finally, I will show the export. One click generates a full landscape PDF report — every person, their rank, status, and section assignment, formatted and ready to hand to leadership. No spreadsheet, no manual entry, no waiting.
